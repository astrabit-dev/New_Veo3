# 94. 다큐·자연 프롬프트 20선

## 다큐·자연 프롬프트 작성 원칙
- 관찰하는 것처럼, 방해하지 않는 것처럼
- 자연 조명만 사용
- 내레이터 목소리는 조용하고 경이롭게
- 동물/자연의 주체성을 존중하는 시점

---

**#1 북극곰 어미와 새끼**
```
Documentary footage of a polar bear mother walking across sea ice with two small cubs following closely behind. The ice is blindingly white, the sky that particular pale arctic blue. The cubs occasionally tumble and slide — their coordination is still developing — and the mother waits patiently each time, continuing only when both are with her again. Telephoto lens from a distance. Natural overcast arctic light. A gentle narrator says: "The first months outside the den will determine everything." Sound: wind, ice, the faint calls of the cubs.
```

**#2 심해 발광 생물**
```
Documentary deep-sea footage of a bioluminescent zone — shot with special low-light cameras in the absolute darkness of the deep ocean. Multiple species of creatures drift through the frame, each creating its own light: long tentacled siphonophores, pulsing jellyfish with coordinated bioluminescence, a dragonfish revealing its light organs as it banks past the camera. The narrator speaks with quiet reverence: "In the deepest part of the ocean, in complete darkness, life invented its own light." No artificial light — only the creatures' own.
```

**#3 사하라의 황혼**
```
A documentary-style timelapse of a single day in the Sahara Desert — from the cold purple pre-dawn through the savage midday heat (visible in the air distortion above the dunes) to the extraordinary orange sunset. At the edge of the frame throughout: a single desert fox, barely visible in the day, more active as the light cools, fully animated by dusk. The narrator notes: "The desert is not empty. It is waiting for you to leave." Natural light changes only. Minimal score.
```

**#4 벌집 내부**
```
Macro documentary footage inside a beehive — the extraordinary geometric precision of honeycomb cells, some capped with wax, some open and half-full of honey, some containing larvae in various stages. Hundreds of bees move over the surface with apparent chaos that reveals, on closer observation, to be purposeful coordination. A queen bee moves through the comb — the other bees make way, always facing her. The camera follows her. The sound: the deep collective hum of the hive.
```

**#5 몬순 도착**
```
The arrival of monsoon rains at a parched Indian city — documentary-style. The sky darkens remarkably fast from a bright hot blue to a dramatic purple-black wall advancing from the horizon. People on a rooftop watch it come. Children in the street below are playing in the last of the dry heat. Then the smell — that distinct pre-monsoon petrichor scent is visible in people's reactions before the rain arrives. Then it hits: instantly drenching, warm, a relief so profound people laugh and raise their arms.
```

**#6 가을 철새 이동**
```
A murmuration of starlings at dusk — thousands of birds moving as a single fluid organism across a fading orange sky, their collective form constantly reshaping: a sphere, a ribbon, a wave, a funnel. The camera is stationary, the birds filling and leaving the frame continuously. The sound: the collective whoosh of thousands of wings, a sound like weather. A narrator: "No individual bird leads. Each follows its seven nearest neighbors. The intelligence is in the whole." Blue hour light.
```

**#7 만년설 녹는 모습**
```
Documentary timelapse of a glacier in retreat, observed over what represents years: the ice pulling back from valley walls that were covered two decades ago, revealing rock freshly scoured by the ancient ice. The meltwater runs bright and cold. In the foreground, plants are beginning to colonize the newly exposed ground — first lichens, then grasses. The glacier recedes in the background. A measured, non-melodramatic narrator marks the change. The sound is meltwater.
```

**#8 문어의 위장**
```
Underwater documentary macro of an octopus on a reef, caught in the process of changing its camouflage. The camera has spotted it before it noticed the lens. Watch in extreme close-up as the skin responds — shifting from mottled brown to the specific grey and white patterns of the coral it's resting on. Then it notices the camera and — over three seconds — becomes the coral surface so completely that it essentially disappears. The narrator: "The animal has 2 million color cells in its skin. It is not hiding. It is becoming something else."
```

**#9 아마존 새벽**
```
The first light entering the Amazon rainforest canopy — the light arrives in visible beams through the layers of leaves, the canopy so dense that what reaches the forest floor is a transformed green-gold. The forest wakes: macaws flying between trees above, a tapir drinking at the forest edge, spider monkeys beginning their morning calls. The sound is extraordinary — a hundred bird species creating a layered complexity of sound. Documentary style, natural light only.
```

**#10 도시 여우**
```
Night documentary of urban foxes — a family living beneath a garden shed in a city suburb. The camera, infrared and very still, observes the den entrance. At 11pm: the vixen emerges, nose testing the air, then moves confidently through the garden. Then three cubs follow. They investigate a compost bin, play with a garden glove, sit under a streetlight watching cars pass. The narrator: "Urban foxes have adapted not just to the city — they have learned it." Street sound in the background.
```

**#11 마다가스카르 카멜레온**
```
Extreme slow motion documentary of a Chameleon hunting — the targeting, the coiling preparation, the extraordinary telescoping tongue that extends in 0.003 seconds to catch an insect three times the chameleon's tongue's resting length. In super slow motion, we can see the tongue's contact pad, sticky and muscular. The insect's position through the chameleon's independently-moving eyes. The whole act in real time is invisible; in slow motion it is one of the most extraordinary things on Earth.
```

**#12 히말라야 티벳 고원**
```
Documentary aerial of the Tibetan plateau at high altitude — an immense, nearly flat landscape above the clouds, stretching to impossibly distant mountain peaks. A herd of Tibetan antelope moves across the plateau in the early morning cold, their breath visible. The scale is staggering — the herd, large in number, is a small thread across an infinite horizontal canvas. The narrator speaks about altitude, adaptation, and the animals that have made this extreme environment home. Wind is the only constant sound.
```

**#13 폭풍 추적자**
```
A storm-chasing documentary crew drives toward a supercell tornado in the American Great Plains. From inside the vehicle: the crew monitors radar on a laptop while watching the forming tornado out the front window. The farmland on either side of the highway is absolutely flat and green. The tornado is distant, dark, unmistakable — a long funnel reaching from black clouds to earth. The driver accelerates. The vehicles in the convoy keep in contact by radio. The tornado turns and they turn with it.
```

**#14 산호 백화 현상**
```
Documentary footage of coral bleaching in the Great Barrier Reef — a section of reef that was, months ago, a riot of color, and is now stark white. Ghost coral, still architecturally beautiful, glowing white in the blue water. One fish swims through, its colors startling against the white. Very occasionally, a small patch of color remains — a polyp that survived. The camera explores this quietly, without narration, for a moment. Then the narrator speaks: "The reef is not dead. But it is suffering. And it is watching us watch it."
```

**#15 야행성 영장류**
```
Infrared documentary footage of slow lorises in a Southeast Asian rainforest at night — their enormous reflective eyes glowing in the camera's light. They move very slowly through the branches, their grip remarkable. One stops to investigate a flower, finding nectar with a specialized tongue. The camera is very close — the loris doesn't seem to mind, or perhaps it's assessing. It looks directly into the lens with those enormous night-adapted eyes. The narrator: "The slow loris is one of the only venomous primates. It is also one of the most illegally trafficked."
```

**#16 화산 용암과 바다**
```
Documentary of active lava flowing into the ocean on the Big Island of Hawaii — the lava meets the water and instantly creates enormous steam clouds, the air white and mineral-smelling. In slow motion, individual lava flows reach the surf — the water recoils, then engulfs, instantly cooling and hardening the outer surface while the interior continues to flow. New land is being created in real time. A narrator: "This island is still being built. The process has not stopped in millions of years."
```

**#17 사하라 사막 모래폭풍**
```
Time-lapse documentary of a haboob — a wall of sand two kilometers high — approaching a desert city at the edge of the Sahara. From above: the sand wall is a coherent front, advancing at 50km/h. In the city: people at street level see the brown wall on the horizon and begin to move. Animals react first — birds fly away, camels turn their backs. In under ten minutes, the city is engulfed in red-brown darkness. Then the wall passes. The city is covered in fine sand, everything transformed.
```

**#18 남극 펭귄 콜로니**
```
Aerial documentary of an Emperor penguin colony in Antarctica during winter — thousands of penguins huddled together in organized formation in winds of minus fifty degrees. From the air, the colony looks like a single breathing organism, the penguins at the edges rotating slowly inward, the warm ones moving out, everyone cycling through the center. A single penguin makes its way across the ice from the ocean, carrying the winter fish for a chick. The chick recognizes its parent's call among thousands. They find each other.
```

**#19 우기의 아프리카**
```
Documentary of the Serengeti during the brief rains — the savanna transforming from ochre dust to green almost faster than the eye can believe. Timelapse shows the grass emerging over days, the animals following the growth front. Wildebeest appear from the south in numbers that make the plain look like a moving carpet. The sky is dramatic: daily thunderstorms, after which the light is extraordinary. A lion family rests under an acacia, completely comfortable, the wildebeest grazing within sprinting distance.
```

**#20 빛 공해와 별**
```
A split-screen documentary: on one side, a night sky above a city — orange glow, few stars visible. On the other: the same sky above wilderness — the Milky Way in full. The camera slowly zooms into the wilderness sky until individual star clusters are visible. Then: a first-person perspective of someone experiencing a truly dark sky for the first time — their face turned up, mouth slightly open, completely still. The narrator: "Ninety percent of people alive today have never seen the Milky Way. This is new. This has never happened before in human history."
```
