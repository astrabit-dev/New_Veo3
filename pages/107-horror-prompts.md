# 89. 공포·스릴러 프롬프트 20선

## 공포 프롬프트 작성 원칙
- 보여주지 않는 것이 더 무섭다
- 소리를 세밀하게 묘사 (침묵도 소리다)
- 느린 움직임이 빠른 움직임보다 더 불안하다
- 일상적인 공간을 위협적으로 만들기

---

**#1 빈 놀이터**
```
A children's playground at 3am, completely empty, lit by a single malfunctioning streetlight that flickers rhythmically. A swing begins moving on its own — not in wind, with deliberate rhythm, as if someone is sitting on it. The camera approaches slowly from across the wet grass. As it gets closer, the swing gradually slows. By the time the camera reaches the playground equipment, the swing is perfectly still. The streetlight flickers off. One second of total darkness. Then back on. Something is different. Audio: only the creak of the swing chain, distant traffic, and silence.
```

**#2 지하실 계단**
```
The view from the top of a dark basement staircase, looking down. The light switch is at the bottom. At the bottom of the stairs, in the farthest corner of the basement, barely visible in the ambient darkness, something is standing. It's too dark to see clearly — just a shape, a person-sized shape, that wasn't there before. The shape doesn't move. The camera doesn't move. The shape doesn't move. Thirty seconds of absolute silence, both waiting. Then the basement light switch clicks on by itself.
```

**#3 거울 미로**
```
A figure walks through a hall of mirrors in an old fairground at night, holding a small flashlight. The mirrors multiply the light and the figure into hundreds of versions. She stops. Looks carefully at all the reflections. All of them have stopped, too. All of them are looking at her. She turns 90 degrees. The reflections turn with her. All except one — one reflection, behind and to the left, that turns the other way and slowly walks away through its own mirror.
```

**#4 전화벨**
```
A woman alone in a house at night answers a phone call. Silence on the other end. "Hello?" she says. More silence. Then — very quietly — breathing. Slow, deliberate, unhurried breathing. She doesn't hang up. Neither does the caller. She moves to the window and looks out. The street is empty. She checks the door — locked. Checks the windows. Locked. She goes back to the phone. Still breathing. She says: "Where are you?" The breathing stops. A door opens somewhere inside the house.
```

**#5 인형의 집**
```
A large Victorian dollhouse sits on a table in a child's bedroom, lit by a nightlight. The dollhouse is an exact replica of the room it's in. Including the bed. Including the child sleeping in the bed. The miniature child in the dollhouse bed sits up. The real child continues sleeping. The miniature child's head slowly turns toward the camera. Its painted face shows no expression. It raises one tiny hand. Very slowly. And waves.
```

**#6 숲속 집**
```
A remote cabin in the woods at night. All lights on inside, warm glow through the windows. From outside, we can see a man reading in the living room — comfortable, unaware. The camera pulls back through the trees to reveal, one by one, the silhouettes of figures standing between the trees. Five. Eight. Twelve. All standing perfectly still. All facing the cabin. The man inside turns a page. The figures don't move. The man gets up to make tea. The figures take one step forward, simultaneously.
```

**#7 바다 속 무언가**
```
Clear open ocean in daylight. A swimmer floats on the surface, looking at the sky. The camera is below, looking up through the water at the swimmer's silhouette against the bright surface. The swimmer is relaxed, unaware. Thirty meters below the swimmer, in the deep blue, a shape is visible. It is enormous. It is not moving. It is looking up. The swimmer kicks lazily, begins to swim toward the boat in the distance. The shape below begins, very slowly, to ascend.
```

**#8 복도 끝**
```
A long hospital corridor, fluorescent lights, 4am. A nurse walks briskly toward the camera from the far end. As she walks, the lights behind her begin to flicker out, one by one, plunging each section of the corridor into darkness as she passes. She doesn't notice — she's looking at a clipboard. The darkness follows her, gets closer. She reaches the nurses' station and looks up. The corridor behind her is completely dark. She turns back to her clipboard. The darkness sits at the edge of the light, thirty feet away. Something in it makes a sound like a breath.
```

**#9 사진 속 낯선 사람**
```
A woman scrolls through old family photos on her phone. Holidays, birthdays, ordinary days. She stops. In a family photo from fifteen years ago — a backyard barbecue — there is a figure standing at the fence at the back of the yard. A man. Standing completely still while everyone else is in motion. She enlarges the photo. His face is clear and perfectly detailed. She has never seen this man before. She scrolls to other photos. Other barbecues. Other gatherings. The same man, always at the periphery. Always still. Always watching.
```

**#10 비가 오지 않는 날**
```
Two people shelter under an umbrella in a busy outdoor market during heavy rain. A few feet away, a woman stands in the rain, not moving, getting completely soaked. She looks up at the sky. Then she turns and looks directly at the camera — looks through it, at us. The rain falls on her at a different angle from everyone else. She is standing in what appears to be her own private rain, separate from the weather around her. Nobody else can see her. She smiles.
```

**#11 박제된 방**
```
A woman is shown an empty apartment to rent. Everything looks normal. Then she notices: on the mantelpiece, a family photo — of her family. Her parents, her. Ten years ago. She doesn't remember the photo being taken. She looks closer. Everyone in the photo is looking at the camera except her father — he is looking at something to the left, outside the frame. Something in the apartment. She slowly turns to the left. In the corner of the room, barely visible in the shadow, an old chair. A man sitting in it.
```

**#12 층간소음**
```
A woman lies awake in her apartment at 3am. From the ceiling — from the apartment above — slow footsteps pace the floor. Creak. Pause. Creak. Pause. She knows the apartment above is empty. She has asked the landlord. She lies very still. The footsteps stop directly above her head. Silence. Then, slowly, something begins to tap the ceiling above her — directly, precisely above her face. Tap. Tap. Tap. Like a knock, but from above. From inside the ceiling.
```

**#13 병원 엘리베이터**
```
A man in a hospital late at night enters an empty elevator. The doors close. At the next floor, the doors open — the corridor is identical to the one he left. He didn't press any buttons. He steps out cautiously. At the far end of the corridor, a patient in a hospital gown is walking away from him. He follows. At the end of the corridor, the patient enters an elevator. He reaches it as the doors close. The floor indicator above the door goes up: 1, 2, 3, 4... keeps going. The hospital only has four floors.
```

**#14 동굴 탐험**
```
Two spelunkers deep inside a cave system, moving through a passage barely wide enough to squeeze through. Headlamps light the way. The passage opens into a chamber. On the cave walls: handprints. Thousands of them, ancient ochre handprints covering every surface. Then one of the spelunkers stops. Points. In the center of all the ancient prints — one set of handprints that looks fresh. The ochre not yet dry. Something is still in this cave. Their headlamps both flicker, then go out simultaneously.
```

**#15 비어있는 마을**
```
A traveler drives down a highway into a small town and finds it completely empty. Cars parked. Shops unlocked. Food still on tables in the diner, coffee still warm. She gets out and walks through the empty main street. Wind moves a newspaper. A traffic light changes for nobody. She enters the diner. Finds fifty cups of coffee on fifty tables, all still steaming. As if everyone simply stood up and left, seconds ago. She goes back to her car. All four tires have been slashed.
```

**#16 어린이 그림**
```
A father finds a folder of drawings his daughter made. Sweet, childlike drawings of their house, their family, their neighborhood. He smiles — until he reaches the last drawing. It shows their kitchen. Drawn with the same childlike style. Except: in the kitchen, behind the father who is reading a newspaper and doesn't know, the drawing shows a tall thin figure standing in the corner. The same figure appears in the next drawing. And the next. Always behind someone. Always where the family isn't looking. He asks his daughter who the tall person is. She says: "That's my friend who watches you when you sleep."
```

**#17 안개 속 해변**
```
A woman walks alone on a foggy beach at dawn. Visibility: twenty meters. She can hear the waves but barely see the water. Ahead in the fog: a shape. She stops. It resolves into a person, standing at the water's edge, facing the sea. She calls out. The figure doesn't respond. She approaches. Gets close enough to see it's a woman in old-fashioned clothing, standing completely still, ankles in the surf. She touches the figure's shoulder. The figure turns around. The face is her own. Except aged thirty years. The older version of herself looks at her with profound sadness and says: "Don't go back."
```

**#18 수면 마비**
```
A woman wakes suddenly in the dark, completely unable to move — classic sleep paralysis. She can only move her eyes. In the corner of her room, a figure stands. She has had sleep paralysis before. She knows the figure isn't real. She tells herself this. The figure takes one step toward the bed. She continues telling herself this. The figure sits on the edge of the bed. The mattress actually compresses with real weight. She stops telling herself it isn't real.
```

**#19 유품**
```
A man sorts through his late grandmother's belongings in her old house. In a box of her things, he finds a phone. Not an antique — a modern smartphone, charging cable still attached. He powers it on. The last app open is the camera. He sees the most recent photo. It shows this room. This box. Him, from behind, bending over the box, sorting through it. The photo is time-stamped: forty minutes from now.
```

**#20 마지막 열차**
```
The last train of the night, mostly empty. One other passenger, far down the car — a man who doesn't look up when the protagonist boards. The train moves. The protagonist looks out the window at the dark tunnels. When he looks back, the man has moved — now closer, several rows. He didn't hear him move. He looks away again, just seconds. Looks back. The man is in the next row. Protagonist stands up. Looks directly at the other man. The man's face is wrong — not violent, not threatening, just profoundly, fundamentally wrong in a way that can't be articulated. The train stops at a station. The protagonist gets off. Watches through the window as the train pulls away. The man is standing, watching him from the train. Still wrong. Getting smaller as the train disappears into the dark.
```
