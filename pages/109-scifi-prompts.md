# 91. SF·판타지 프롬프트 20선

## SF·판타지 프롬프트 작성 원칙
- 세계관 디테일로 현실감 부여
- 스케일의 대비 (개인 vs 광대한 우주/세계)
- 마법/기술의 물리적 효과를 구체적으로 묘사
- 경이로움과 위험이 공존하는 장면

---

**#1 첫 번째 웜홀 통과**
```
The bridge of a sleek spacecraft approaching a massive wormhole — a swirling aperture of impossible colors (deep violet, electric green, impossible white) hanging in the black of space. The crew watches through the panoramic window, expressions mixing awe and fear. The pilot's hands are steady on the controls. As the ship enters the wormhole, light bends around the hull — the stars stretch into lines, reality seems to fold. Then complete white. Then: an entirely different star field. They made it. Silence on the bridge. Then someone laughs with pure relief.
```

**#2 마법 도서관**
```
An infinite library where the books fly on their own — thousands of books drifting in lazy arcs through cavernous stone halls lit by floating orbs of warm light. A young scholar reaches up and a book floats into her hands. She opens it — the text rearranges itself as she reads, responding to her understanding. Spiral staircases lead up to upper levels that seem to continue beyond the ceiling into other dimensions. The library hums softly with accumulated knowledge.
```

**#3 신들의 회의**
```
On top of a mountain above the clouds, ancient gods of various mythologies sit around a rough stone table: a thunderstorm god, an ocean deity, a fire spirit, a nature goddess. They are arguing — politely but with devastating intensity. The weather around the mountain responds to their moods: lightning from the storm god's side, waves crashing far below from the ocean deity's frustration. The nature goddess finally raises her hand for quiet. They all turn. She has brought something small in her cupped hands. Something alive. Something fragile. "This," she says, "is what we're arguing about."
```

**#4 빛의 기사**
```
In a vast dark castle, a knight whose armor is made entirely of light — not reflecting light, but composed of it — walks slowly through shadow-filled halls. Each step illuminates the floor beneath. Darkness retreats from her presence. At the end of the hall: a door sealed with chains of shadow. She reaches out, and where her hand of light touches the shadow-chains, they dissolve. The door opens onto a landscape of pure, blinding dawn light.
```

**#5 외계 도시 첫 조우**
```
Human explorers in spacesuits stand at the edge of a canyon, looking across at an alien city that has grown organically — structures that look like crystals, like coral, like living things, glowing from within with bioluminescent colors. It's clearly inhabited — movement visible inside the structures. A scout team of aliens notices the humans from across the canyon. Both groups stop completely still, separated by hundreds of meters of open air, just looking at each other. First contact. Nobody moves first.
```

**#6 시간 마법사**
```
A city street, frozen in time — a taxi in mid-splash through a puddle, a child mid-laugh, an umbrella caught by wind halfway through its arc. A robed figure walks through the frozen moment, examining things carefully. She reaches the frozen taxi, looks at the driver's frozen expression, touches his shoulder gently. He unfreezes — alone in his bubble of time, confused. "What year is it?" she asks. He answers. She nods, a small sad expression, touches his shoulder again. He freezes again, mid-word. She continues walking through the frozen city.
```

**#7 드래곤의 알 부화**
```
In the depths of an ancient volcanic cave, a dragon egg the size of a boulder sits in a nest of cooled lava rock. The egg pulses with internal light — warm gold from within. Cracks begin to form. The cracks widen. A small scaled snout appears. Then an eye — enormous, golden, ancient before the creature is even hatched. It looks around at the cave, at the world, for the first time. A sound — somewhere between a chirp and a thunder crack. The eye blinks. This is the beginning of something enormous.
```

**#8 해저 왕국**
```
The royal court of an underwater kingdom: a vast throne room where bioluminescent jellyfish drift as living chandeliers, and the walls are living coral shaped into architectural forms. The queen — half human, half something else, with fins and cold intelligent eyes — presides over her court of sea creatures and half-human subjects. Through the massive transparent dome above: the dark ocean stretching up to a faint surface light far above. This is a complete civilization, as complex as anything on land.
```

**#9 우주 정원사**
```
A colossal being moves slowly between galaxies, their hands cupped around a nascent star — a baby sun the size of a skyscraper to us, tiny to them. They breathe on it gently, adding something. The star brightens, stabilizes. Around it, they arrange the gravitational currents that will one day bring planets into orbit. They work with the careful attention of someone planting a garden. In the background: countless finished solar systems, already inhabited by tiny civilizations they may never notice.
```

**#10 마법 전쟁 후**
```
A battlefield after a war fought entirely with magic. Where fire spells landed: permanent glass deserts. Where ice magic struck: frozen forests of crystal. Where dark magic was used: shadows that are somehow solid, unmovable, permanent. A surviving mage walks through the aftermath, staff in hand, cataloging the damage. She reaches the center of the battle — where the opposing archmages met — and finds a sphere of pure silence, twenty meters across, where the magic cancelled itself out completely. She steps inside it. Total peace. She sits down in the middle of it and puts her face in her hands.
```

**#11 별을 먹는 생물**
```
In deep space, an entity the size of a solar system moves through the void, trailing light like a comet. As it passes a yellow star, it opens what might be a mouth — a geometric pattern of energy that expands outward — and draws the star's energy into itself. The star dims and contracts. Not destroyed — just dimmed, harvested. The entity moves on. In the stellar neighborhood nearby, a small fleet of human ships watches from a safe distance. Small, fragile, awed. Nobody says anything on the bridge for a long time.
```

**#12 예언의 결정체**
```
In a mountain monastery, a monk carries a small glowing crystal through long corridors lined with other crystals — each containing a different prophecy, each faintly glowing with different colors. He is adding a new one. In his crystal: a vision of a child being born in a distant city. He sets it in its place on the shelf. It pulses once — connects to the others. A network of faint light threads between the crystals as they recognize each other. The monk bows to them all and leaves the room in darkness.
```

**#13 나무 위의 도시**
```
A civilization built entirely in the canopy of a forest of impossibly vast trees — each trunk the width of a city block, the canopy so high that clouds form below the highest platforms. Between the trees: bridges of woven living wood, rope and light, some broad enough for markets, some just wide enough for one person. The light filters green through layers of leaves above. Whole generations are born and die without ever touching the ground. Far below, barely visible: the forest floor, which has become mythology.
```

**#14 생각 도서관**
```
An archivist in a future civilization walks down corridors where, on the shelves, instead of books, there are crystallized human memories — small glowing objects, each a different color, each containing a complete human experience. She finds what she's looking for and presses the memory to her temple. Someone else's life floods through her: childhood in a different era, love in a world that no longer exists, the feeling of rain on a face that has been gone for a thousand years. She replaces the memory carefully. Continues on.
```

**#15 마지막 요정**
```
The last fairy in a world that has forgotten magic sits at the base of a massive industrial structure, completely invisible to the hundreds of humans passing by on their way to work. She's very small — the size of a hummingbird. She watches them with ancient eyes. One small girl — walking behind her parents, not yet grown out of believing in things — turns her head and looks directly at the fairy. Their eyes meet. The girl gasps. The fairy puts one tiny finger to her lips. The girl nods. This is their secret.
```

**#16 우주선 묘지**
```
A graveyard of spacecraft from a dozen civilizations, drifting in the gravitational balance point between two stars. Ships of impossible design: some crystalline, some organic-looking, some made of technology completely unrecognizable. An explorer drifts among them in a small personal craft, cataloging. She finds one — ancient beyond measure — that shows signs of having been lived in for generations. A civilization that traveled between stars without ever landing anywhere. The ship itself was their world.
```

**#17 마법 문자 학교**
```
A school where students learn to write in living script — letters that can walk off the page, words that rearrange themselves if written incorrectly, sentences that can be sent to distant places on their own. A young student writes a letter to his family. As he writes, the letters glow softly. He folds the paper and releases it — it unfolds, becomes a bird of words, and flies out the window. His teacher watches. Notes something in her grade book. The paper-bird disappears over the rooftop.
```

**#18 두 달의 세계**
```
A planet that orbits a binary star system, with two moons of different sizes and colors — one gold, one deep blue. The sky at sunset is therefore always extraordinary: two suns setting, two moons rising, the sky a layered spectrum of colors impossible on Earth. On a hillside, a family watches the nightly spectacle together. Every night. Because on this world, the sky is never the same twice, and every sunset is a reason to stop what you're doing.
```

**#19 바다의 기억**
```
A scientist in the deep ocean discovers that the ocean has memory — that the water itself retains impressions of everything that has ever moved through it. She finds a way to read it. With special gloves, she passes her hands through a deep cold current and sees: ships from ten centuries past, schools of fish long extinct, the passing of things no human has ever seen. The ocean has recorded everything. She looks at the water around her with completely different eyes.
```

**#20 판타지 시장 마감 시간**
```
A magical market at dusk — merchants packing up their impossible wares: bottled dreams, crystallized laughter, bags of solid silence, compasses that point toward love instead of north. A young customer rushes in at closing time, desperate. She has one coin. The merchants are packing up but pause to look at her. What does she need? She holds out her coin. "I want to give this to whoever needs it most." The merchants all look at each other. One very old merchant reaches into his nearly packed box and hands her something small. She opens her hand. The most ordinary object imaginable. She understands. She pockets it and goes home.
```
