# 88. 로맨스·드라마 프롬프트 20선

## 로맨스·드라마 프롬프트 작성 원칙
- 말보다 행동으로 감정을 표현
- 감성적인 조명과 색상 설정
- 두 사람 사이의 공간과 거리를 활용
- 소소한 물리적 접촉이 감정을 전달

---

**#1 첫 만남 — 도서관**
```
A young woman reaches for a book on a high library shelf — her fingers brush the spine of a book she can't quite reach. A man's hand appears beside hers and easily retrieves the book. She turns in surprise. He holds it out with a small, slightly nervous smile. Their eyes meet. Both of them notice, at the same moment, that they're holding the same book. Warm library light, the smell of old books implied in every frame, soft classical music from somewhere unseen.
```

**#2 빗속 고백**
```
A man stands in the rain outside a coffee shop window, looking at the woman inside who doesn't know he's there. She laughs at something her friend said, unaware. He watches for a long moment, getting soaked, trying to find his courage. He reaches for the door handle. His hand rests there, doesn't push. Then she looks up — catches his eyes through the glass. His hand pushes the door open. She watches him come in, dripping wet.
```

**#3 이별의 아침**
```
Two people in a sun-filled apartment. She's packing a suitcase on the bed. He stands in the doorway, hands in his pockets, not helping, not leaving. She folds a sweater — his sweater, left at her place months ago. She holds it a moment, then puts it in the suitcase. He sees. She looks up. Neither speaks. Morning light is beautiful and cruel. The taxi downstairs honks once.
```

**#4 오래된 편지**
```
A woman in her 60s finds a bundle of letters tied with ribbon in a box in the attic. She unties the ribbon with careful hands. Opens the first letter. Reads. A small smile appears — the kind that belongs to young people she no longer is. She reads another. And another. We see fragments of the past through her changing expression: joy, grief, longing, peace. She ties the letters back up carefully. Holds them against her chest. Closes her eyes.
```

**#5 재회 — 기차역**
```
A crowded train station, commuters flowing everywhere. A woman stands still in the middle of the current, a small bag over her shoulder, scanning faces. Then she sees him — at the far end of the platform, also scanning. Their eyes meet across fifty meters of moving people. Both of them stop. The crowd flows around them both like a river around two stones. Then they both start walking toward each other. Slowly at first, then faster.
```

**#6 요리와 음악**
```
A man dances slowly in a small kitchen while cooking, alone, happy. He doesn't notice that his partner has come home early and is watching from the doorway with the most tender expression imaginable. He spins — sees her. Freezes. She's grinning. He holds out his hand. She takes it. They dance together in the tiny kitchen, the food slowly overcooking on the stove. Neither cares.
```

**#7 병원 대기실**
```
A man in casual clothes sits in a hospital waiting room, head in his hands. He's been there for hours — coffee cup empty, jacket over the seat beside him. A woman he knows slightly — a neighbor, a colleague — sits beside him. They don't know each other well. She doesn't say anything. After a moment, she puts her hand over his. He looks up, surprised. She gives him a small nod. He doesn't pull his hand away.
```

**#8 비밀 결혼 제안**
```
Two people on a hiking trail at sunset, reaching the peak of a small hill overlooking an incredible valley. He stops, pretending to be out of breath. She walks ahead to the viewpoint. When she turns back to comment on the view, he's on one knee, a small ring box open in his hand, trying to look calm and failing entirely. She puts both hands over her mouth. The valley glows orange behind him.
```

**#9 마지막 댄스**
```
An elderly couple dances slowly in their living room to a song playing on the radio — a song that means something to them that we don't know and don't need to know. They know every move of each other's bodies. His hand on her waist. Her head on his shoulder. The room around them is full of decades of living together: photos on the mantle, worn furniture, afternoon light. They barely move. Just sway.
```

**#10 거리두기**
```
A couple sits at opposite ends of a sofa. Between them: distance, a TV neither is watching, the remnants of an argument. She stares at her phone without looking at it. He stares at the ceiling. Time passes. His foot shifts almost imperceptibly toward her. Her pinky finger uncurls on the cushion between them. Neither looks at the other. His foot touches hers. She doesn't move away. The TV light flickers.
```

**#11 창문 너머**
```
A woman in an apartment notices for the first time that the man in the apartment across the street is reading the same book she is. She holds her copy up to the window. He looks up. Sees. Holds his copy up. They're on different pages — she's further ahead. He holds up five fingers — five chapters ahead of him. She laughs. So does he, across the distance. She holds up one finger: one chapter at a time.
```

**#12 공항 귀환**
```
Arrivals hall of an international airport. A child sits on her father's shoulders, holding a handwritten sign: "WELCOME HOME MOM." People stream past. Then — a woman with a carry-on, disheveled from a long flight, rounding the corner. She sees the sign first. Then the child. Then her husband. She breaks into a run. The child shouts "MAMA" at the top of his lungs. People around them smile.
```

**#13 빈 집**
```
A man walks through an empty apartment for the last time — the furniture gone, the walls bare, only rectangular shadows where pictures used to hang. He pauses at a scuff mark on the kitchen wall — a height measurement written in pencil: a child's name and date from years ago. He photographs it with his phone. Stands there for a moment. Touches it gently. Then walks to the door, turns off the light.
```

**#14 졸업식 날**
```
A mother watches her daughter receive her diploma from the back of the graduation auditorium. The hall is full of noise and celebration. But the mother is completely still, watching, tears streaming freely, a smile so wide it might break her face. The father beside her tries to hold the camera steady, also crying. The daughter walks back to her seat, scans the crowd, finds her parents. Waves.
```

**#15 별밤 아래**
```
Two friends lie on a car hood in the middle of nowhere, miles from the city, looking at a sky so full of stars it seems impossible. They're talking — we can see their mouths move but hear only the crickets and the distant highway. One points at a constellation. The other disagrees — points differently. They argue lightly. Then silence. Comfortable, full silence. The Milky Way stretches above them.
```

**#16 설거지**
```
A couple washes dishes together in a small apartment kitchen. He washes, she dries. The mundane rhythm of it. She says something that makes him laugh — really laugh, hands still in the soapy water. He flicks soap bubbles at her. She retaliates. It escalates into a minor water fight that soaks the counter and the floor. They look at the mess, then at each other. Start laughing again.
```

**#17 친구의 결혼식**
```
At a wedding reception, two people are seated at the same table who don't know each other. The speeches begin. She's moved — a tear escapes. He notices and very quietly, without a word, slides his napkin slightly toward her. She notices. Looks at him. He's not looking at her — watching the speaker with complete innocence. She takes the napkin. Uses it. Puts it back. Neither mentions it. Both smiling slightly.
```

**#18 환자와 의사**
```
A doctor sits on the edge of a hospital bed beside an elderly patient who has just received difficult news. The forms and the procedure are done. The room is quiet. The doctor doesn't leave immediately. She sits. The patient looks out the window. After a long moment, the patient says something small — not about medicine. The doctor responds. They talk. Just two people, for a moment, in the late afternoon light.
```

**#19 사진 한 장**
```
A man finds a photograph in an old coat — himself, twenty years younger, with a woman whose face he knows but whose story we don't. He sits down heavily on the bed. Studies the photo. The younger version of himself looks impossibly young and impossibly happy. He runs a thumb across her face in the photograph. Then carefully puts the photo in his wallet, not in a drawer. He gets up. Puts the coat on.
```

**#20 첫 아이의 밤**
```
New parents — exhausted beyond description — sit on the floor of a nursery at 3am, backs against the crib. The baby has finally, finally gone to sleep. They are too tired to move. They lean against each other. He's asleep within thirty seconds. She looks at him, then at the crib, then back. Laughs very quietly to herself — the laugh of someone who has no idea what they're doing and is okay with that. She closes her eyes.
```
